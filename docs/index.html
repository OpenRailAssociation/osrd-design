Method

03.10.2023

Experience building other design systems, learned best practices, reviewing and comparing prominent, exhaustive design systems, qualitative testing with users.

Aspirations

03.10.2023

Reflecting the "study tool" nature of OSRD. It belongs in a library, an engineering office or in a workshop, rather than in a shopping mall or a candy store. Adding more depth and presence to interactive elements than the flat design trend.

Constraints

03.10.2023

Making OSRD accessible: high contrast in colours and sufficiently large active zones for interactive elements will have to be respected as much as possible, while maintaining efficiency on the target screen size.

Discoverability: OSRD is an industrial tool, not a public facing website where users have to figure out everything on their own. The axiom “UX is like a joke; if you need to explain it, it isn’t that good” doesn’t apply in such context. Still, we must do everything we can to figure out smart and obvious ways for users to accomplish their tasks.

Making OSRD last: timeless aesthetics, no cool kids gimmicks.

OSRD isn’t a brand: no guidelines for colours, fonts and tone to start with. Which can be paralysing, but it gives us more wiggle room to design a tool we’re going to love.

Colours

03.10.2023

Role in the interface

Colours provide semantic clues to users, so consistency is key. They're informing about the context they're in, about what they can—can't—should—shouldn't do, helping organise complex sets of informations.

In tools like OSRD, text is everywhere, so colours affect everything. Ambience, text, communicating state of interactive elements.

Common issues

Colours are culturally subjective.

Colours are not physiologically perceived the same way by everyone.

Contrasts are not equal from one tint to another at the same saturation and luminance values. USWDS' magic number.

Palettes

Colour palettes are not just a container where to pick references, they're a tool to ensure visual harmony. Design systems sometimes provide a handful of colours to work with, sometimes 10 variations for each tint. OSRD Design System will follow the latter situation, which by experience allows for more flexibility while maintaining consistency on the long term.

OSRD specifics

The brand-less nature of OSRD frees us from reflecting a specific set of pre-existing values. The primary colour, as an example, is usually derived from the primary brand colour.
